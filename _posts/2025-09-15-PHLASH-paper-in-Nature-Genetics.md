Our [PHLASH paper](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41588-025-02323-x), _Accelerated Bayesian inference of population size history from recombining sequence data_, has (finally!) appeared in _Nature Genetics_. The paper introduces a new Bayesian method for demographic inference from whole-genome sequence data that is fast, scalable, phase-invariant, and equipped with uncertainty quantification. A key technical ingredient is a new algorithm for computing the score function of a coalescent hidden Markov model at essentially the same cost as evaluating the likelihood, which makes full Bayesian inference practical on modern hardware.
