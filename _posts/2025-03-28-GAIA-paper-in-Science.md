The [GAIA paper](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adp4642), has now appeared in _Science_. Huge credit to Michael Grundler for leading this project! GAIA uses georeferenced tree sequences to infer where shared human genetic ancestors lived through time, yielding a spatiotemporal view of ancestry rather than a single static label. It was a fun collaboration, and I think the paper opens up some interesting new directions at the interface of population genetics, genealogy, and spatial inference.
